Fruits and Vegetables

Fruits: Botanically, a fruit is the seed-bearing structure that develops from the ovary of a flowering plant. They naturally contain seeds and generally have a sweet or tart flavor.Vegetables: Vegetables encompass all other edible parts of a plant, including roots, stems, leaves, and flower buds. They usually feature a milder, savory taste.

Fruits

Mango

The mango is a popular tropical fruit known as the "King of Fruits". Native to South Asia, it features a distinct smooth skin, sweet juicy flesh, and a central seed. Rich in vitamins and available in hundreds of varieties, it is enjoyed fresh, juiced, or cooked into various regional delicacies

Guava

Guava is a highly nutritious tropical fruit native to Central America, prized for its sweet, musky aroma and juicy, edible-seeded flesh. Typically round or pear-shaped with light green or yellow skin, its inner pulp varies in color and holds powerful antioxidants, dietary fiber, and exceptionally high levels of vitamin C.

Vegetables

Tomato

Tomatoes are highly nutritious fruits from the nightshade family that are globally used as culinary vegetables. Rich in vitamin C and the antioxidant lycopene, they are excellent for heart and skin health. Botanically, they are fruits but are essential ingredients for countless savory dishes, sauces, and salads.

Corn

Corn, also known as maize, is a major cereal grain originally domesticated in Mexico thousands of years ago. It is a versatile crop cultivated globally and used for human food, livestock feed, and biofuel. Highly nutritious and rich in fiber, it remains a staple worldwide

Cucumber

Cucumbers are highly refreshing, nutrient-dense fruits (often treated as vegetables) belonging to the melon family. They are long, cylindrical, and consist of about 95% water. Extremely low in calories, they are widely eaten raw in salads, made into pickles, and provide excellent hydration and essential vitamins like Vitamin K and C
